Explore stories from people living with Cystic Fibrosis (CF) who have a lung transplant. In the stories, they share their journeys from pre-transplant to post and give advice to others living with CF.

Helpful Definitions

FEV1 (forced expiratory volume in one second) is a lung function measurement (a type of pulmonary function test or "PFT"). FEV1 measures the volume of air your lungs can breathe out in one second and is an accurate way to determine how well your lungs function. The FEV1 is an important component of the decision for a CF doctor to recommend lung transplant as a treatment option. FEV1 is also used after lung transplant to determine how well the lung transplant is working.
